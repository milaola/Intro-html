Invention of writing The invention of writing around 3200 BC in ancient Mesopotamia marked a pivotal shift from oral traditions to recorded knowledge, fundamentally transforming human societies by enabling precise record-keeping, administration, and the accumulation of information across generations. Prior to writing, knowledge was transmitted verbally, limiting its scope, accuracy, and permanence stories, laws, and innovations could be lost with the death of storytellers or elders. Cuneiform, the earliest known script developed by the Sumerians in Uruk (modern-day Iraq), began as pictographic tokens for accounting goods like grain and livestock on clay tablets, evolving into a complex system of wedge-shaped impressions representing syllables and concepts
